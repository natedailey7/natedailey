About Mappy

Since the beginning of fall 2020, I’ve been working at the Formal Analysis of Interactive Media (FAIM) Lab at Pomona College, a paid position where I assist in the development of Mappy, a Rust program which interprets pixel data from emulated Nintendo NES games. Although Mappy’s main feature is to produce game maps (linking together different levels and rooms), I specifically have worked on Sprite Blobbing and Avatar Detection features, which involve tracking game sprites and grouping/classifying them based on their movement and relation to user input. In fall 2021, we submitted a paper on Mappy to a small conference called AIIDE (AI and Interactive Digital Entertainment). Not only was the paper accepted, but it also received an award for best paper at the conference.
